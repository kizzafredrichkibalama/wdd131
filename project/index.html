What Is Robotics?

Robotics is the interdisciplinary science that combines engineering, computer science, and mathematics to design, build, program, and operate machines capable of carrying out tasks autonomously or with minimal human guidance.

Modern robots range from microscopic surgical tools to colossal industrial arms and the roving science labs exploring Mars. At their core, all robots share three fundamentals: sensors that perceive the environment, control systems that process that information, and actuators the motors and mechanisms that produce physical movement.

Rapid advances in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and materials science are giving rise to a new generation of robots that learn from experience, collaborate naturally with people, and perform in conditions that were once considered impossible for machines.
