History of Robotics

From Karel Čapek's invention of the word "robot" to today's AI-driven machines — a century of breakthroughs that transformed science fiction into everyday reality.

Key Milestones

The major events that shaped modern robotics, from 1920 to the present day.

1920

The Word "Robot" Is Born

Czech playwright Karel Čapek premieres R.U.R. (Rossum's Universal Robots), coining the word "robot" from the Czech "robota," meaning forced labor.

1942

Asimov's Three Laws

Science fiction author Isaac Asimov publishes his short story "Runaround," introducing the Three Laws of Robotics that would influence robot ethics for decades.

1954

Unimate Patent Filed

American inventor George Devol files a patent for the first programmable robotic arm. His invention, Unimate, would go on to revolutionize industrial manufacturing.

1961

First Industrial Robot Deployed

Unimate begins work on a General Motors assembly line in New Jersey, performing die casting and welding tasks. Industrial robotics is born.

1966

Shakey — The First Mobile AI Robot

Stanford Research Institute develops Shakey, the first robot to combine reasoning, perception, and movement — a milestone for artificial intelligence in robotics.

1973

First 6-Axis Robot Arm

KUKA Robotics in Germany develops FAMULUS, the world's first robot with six electromechanically driven axes — setting the standard for modern industrial robot arms.

1997

Mars Sojourner Lands

NASA's Sojourner becomes the first wheeled vehicle to operate on another planet, proving that robotic exploration of Mars is possible and paving the way for future rovers.

2000

Honda Releases ASIMO

Honda unveils ASIMO, the world's most advanced humanoid robot at the time, capable of walking, climbing stairs, and recognizing faces and voices.

2002

Roomba Launches

iRobot releases the Roomba, the first commercially successful domestic robot. It brings autonomous robotics into millions of homes and sparks the consumer robot industry.

2012

Curiosity Rover Lands on Mars

NASA's Curiosity rover lands in Gale Crater and begins its ongoing mission to study Martian geology and climate, becoming the most capable mobile science lab ever sent to another planet.

2015

Boston Dynamics Reveals Atlas

Boston Dynamics presents Atlas, a hydraulic humanoid robot capable of walking on rough terrain, opening doors, and recovering from falls — ushering in a new era of dynamic robotics.

2021

Ingenuity First Flight on Mars

NASA's Ingenuity helicopter becomes the first powered aircraft to achieve controlled flight on another planet, a landmark for both robotics and aerospace engineering.
